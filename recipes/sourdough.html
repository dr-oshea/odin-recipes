Daniel's White Sourdough Bread

Description

This recipe is for Daniel's standard approach to baking sourdough bread!

It requires a maintained active sourdough starter, and a couple of days work!

This recipe makes two round loaves though ingredients may be halved for a single loaf.

Ingredients

  • Sourdough starter (xxx g)
  • Flour (xxx g)
  • Salt (xxx g)
  • Water

Method

  1. Replenish Starter

    1. Remove starter from fridge two nights before final loaf is to be baked
    2. Empty starter jar to 630g (mass includes jar)
    3. Replenish with 100g of wholemeal flour and 100g of tepid-warm water
    4. Stir to combine. Leave at room teperature overnight, it should hopefully close to double in volume

  2. Create Levain

    1. Add xx g of active starter to a new container
    2. Add xx g of whole meal flour and xx g of white flour
    3. Add xx g of tepid-warm water
    4. Stir to fully combine
    5. Leave levain in a sunny spot for 4 hours and 45 minutes
    6. Replenish Starter according to Stage 1 and return to fridge until next bake

  3. Mix Dough

    1. Set a large bowl on a scale and zero the scale
    2. Add all the levain to the bowl. Zero the scale
    3. Add 600g of tepid-warm water to the bowl
    4. Mix the levain and water to fully dissolve
    5. Add 250g of wholemeal flour and 900g of white flour
    6. Mix to combine and hydrate mix completely
    7. Cover with a damp teatowel and leave for 20 minutes to autolyse

  4. Knead

    1. Add 20g of plain salt
    2. Add a dash of water if needed then mix the salt into the dough
    3. Empty the dough out onto a clean bench
    4. Knead the dough using the slap and fold approach for 5 minutes
    5. Return to bowl, cover with a damp teatowel and encase in a plastic bag
